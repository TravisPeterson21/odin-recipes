Korean BBQ

via GIPHY

Korean BBQ is a style of cooking and eating in which various meats, usually beef, pork, or chicken, are grilled at the table over a gas or charcoal grill. The meat is often marinated in a variety of sauces and spices, giving it a unique and flavorful taste. In addition to the meat, Korean BBQ often includes a variety of side dishes, such as rice, kimchi (spicy fermented vegetables), pickled vegetables, and lettuce leaves. Korean BBQ is typically served family-style, with the grilled meat being shared among the diners. The meat is cooked to the diner's desired level of doneness and then cut into bite-sized pieces with scissors. The meat is then wrapped in lettuce leaves along with the side dishes, creating a flavor-packed bite. Some Korean BBQ restaurants also offer additional dipping sauces and condiments to enhance the flavor of the grilled meat. Korean BBQ is a social and interactive dining experience, with diners cooking their own meat at the table and sharing food with each other. It has become increasingly popular worldwide, with many Korean BBQ restaurants opening in major cities around the world.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450 g) thinly sliced beef (flank steak or ribeye work well)
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup (60 ml) brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons (30 ml) sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons (30 ml) rice wine (mirin)
  • 1 tablespoon (15 ml) minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon (15 ml) minced ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon (1 ml) black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon (15 ml) vegetable oil
  • Green onions, chopped (optional)
  • Sesame seeds (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a medium-sized mixing bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, rice wine, garlic, ginger, and black pepper until the sugar has dissolved.
  2. Add the sliced beef to the bowl, and toss to coat the meat in the marinade. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or up to overnight.
  3. When you're ready to cook the meat, heat a grill pan or outdoor grill to high heat.
  4. Brush the grill with vegetable oil to prevent sticking, then grill the marinated beef for 2-3 minutes per side, or until cooked to your desired level of doneness.
  5. Once the meat is cooked, transfer it to a serving platter and sprinkle with chopped green onions and sesame seeds, if desired.
  6. Serve the grilled beef with lettuce leaves, rice, and other Korean side dishes such as kimchi, pickled vegetables, and dipping sauces.

Note: You can also use pork or chicken for Korean BBQ, and adjust the cooking time accordingly.
